About Me

My name is Nick Pettis, I am 31 and am currently a student in the Georgia Tech Full-Stack Online Coding Boot Camp for the 2nd quarter cohort. I currently live in Fayetteville, GA which is a suburn just south of Atlanta. I have lived in Fayetteville my entire life except for when I went off to college. My first year of college I stayed home and commuted to a junior college, Gordon State College, in Barnesville, GA. It was only about a 40 minute drive so it wasn't that bad. I transferred out after my first year and went to Auburn University in Auburn, AL. I finished up my final three years there earning a BS in accounting. While at Auburn I was a student employee for the football team. I filmed the every football practice and game my final two years of college. Once back home I began working at a local CPA firm in Peachtree City, GA. I ended up working there for six years. While there I attended Clayton State University in Morrow, GA, where I earned my MBA with a concentration in Accounting. I have a background in accounting but am interested starting a new career in computer science.

A few of my strengths include: attention to detail, persistence, and being initiative. Throughout my career in accouting I learned that I will need to pay close attention to detail when looking at financial statements. Missing one number on an income statement could cause my client to lose hundreds if not thousands of dollars. This will be important once I become a developer because it may cost me several hours looking for one mistake in my code if I am not paying close attention. As a result, this may cause the company I am working for to lose a lot of money. Being persistant was important as an accountant and will be just as important as a developer. There were times that I did not know the exact audit or tax rule while I was an accountant but I was able to use different resources to look for my answer. This showed that I was not going to give up because I did not know the answer to something. In the developer world this will be key when trying to write a new code and not knowing the correct tag or code to use. Having initiative will show that I want to learn new things and take on new resonsibilities. When I began my career in accounting I only worked in the tax department but becasue I wanted to expand my knowledge I asked if I could start helping out with audits as well. This allowed me to have more responsibilities but also take on new challenges. Once I start my career as a developer I am sure I will not start on the biggest project available at the time. But as I gain more knowledge I will be able to ask to ask for more challenging work along with more responsibilities.
